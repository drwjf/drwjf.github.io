  3. "Modern Ideas in Chess", by Richard Réti, originally published in 1923, is one of the rare books that transcends the time frame in which it was written. It stands on its own, timeless, one of the true classics in the literature of the game. Richard Réti (28 May 1889 – 6 June 1929) was an Austro-Hungarian, later Czechoslovak chess grandmaster, chess author, and composer of endgame studies. He was one of the principal proponents of hypermodernism in chess.
