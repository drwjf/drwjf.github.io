It stands on its own, timeless, one of the true classics in the literature of the game. Richard Réti (28 May 1889 – 6 June 1929) was an Austro-Hungarian, later Czechoslovak chess grandmaster, chess author, and composer of endgame studies. He was one of the principal proponents of hypermodernism in chess.
