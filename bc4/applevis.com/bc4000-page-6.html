BC4000 - Civilization Model

Submitted by Panais, 17 November, 2021
Last updated on 23 July, 2022
Category
Games

Description of App

The BC4000 APP is a turn-based strategy game. The game has an online mode, a hot seat mode and a single player mode. You take on the role of the ruler of a civilization, attempt to build an empire in competition with other civilizations. The game begins in 4000 BC, before the Bronze Age, and can last through to AD 2100 with Space Age.

Along with the larger tasks of exploration, warfare and diplomacy, you have to make decisions about where to build new cities, which improvements or units to build in each city, when to expand your empire by new settlers, and how to transform the land surrounding the cities for maximum benefit. From time to time your cities may be harassed by units from other nations or barbarians. You should protect borders and maintain zones of control.

You can gain a large advantage if your civilization is the first to learn a particular technology and put it to use in a military or other context. Since only one technology may be researched at any given time, the order in which technologies are chosen makes a considerable difference in the outcome of the game and generally reflects your preferred style of gameplay. Please carefully consider about forms of government, tax rates and research priorities.

You can also build Wonders of the World after obtaining the prerequisite knowledge. These wonders are important achievements of society, science, culture and defense, ranging from the Pyramids and the Great Wall in the Ancient age, to Copernicus' Observatory and Magellan's Expedition in the middle period, up to the Apollo program in the modern era. Each wonder can only be built once in the world, and requires a lot of resources to build, far more than most other city buildings or units. Wonders provide unique benefits to the controlling civilization. For example, Magellan's Expedition increases the movement rate of naval units.

The game can be won by conquering all other civilizations or by winning the space race by reaching the star system of Alpha Centauri.

The strategy logic and user interactive concept are inspired by the original Sid Meier's Civilization. Most graphics, music, map and text materials come from public domain licensed by Creative Commons License or General Public License.

Workers and irrigation

By DMNagel on Sunday, August 28, 2022 - 02:27

Check the mini map from within a city's menu. Move workers to all the tiles on that map and build roads/irrigation. Alternatively, you can just double tap on the work button and the workers will move to those tiles and perform work automatically. As for online games, you can just select the invite a friend option and it should work nicely. there are currently three ways of navigating depending on your situation. First, you can simply use the directional buttons which is perhaps the simplest way of moving a unit. Secondly, you can select the go to button, then explore the map with your finger and double tap on the tile you wish to go to. The unit will find its way to the location automatically, assuming that it's within reason. Thirdly, if you have cities, you can select the go to city button and make use of those menus to order units around from city to city. That's pretty much it. Hope that it was helpful.

Let me explain

By Panais on Sunday, August 28, 2022 - 02:27

There are two things you can do with a tile. Work it and improve it.
To work a tile, you must put your citizens to work on the tile. This is done using the mini map within the city interface.
To work a tile, double tap on it until the game indicates that the selected tile is currently worked by your citizens. When a tile is worked by one of your available citizens, along with the tile coordinates, the game also indicates the resource output of the tile in question. If no output is indicated by the game when you tap on a tile, then the tile is not worked by your citizens.
Improving tiles is another story altogether. The benefit of improving tiles is that when improved, tiles provide extra output to their city when worked by your citizens. To improve tiles you must first build units that are able to provide these improvements. Such units are workers and engineers, although settlers can also do the job all be it slower.
After you have built and selected your workers on the actual map, you must move them upon the tile you want them to improve. When there, just select the improvement you want them to carry out. The main improvements you can build on a tile our irrigation, mine and Road.
Also note that by building certain improvements on a tile, you can also change the nature of the tile. For example if you select to irrigate a forest, the forest will turn into a plane upon which you can then build actual irrigation, in order to gain more food.
